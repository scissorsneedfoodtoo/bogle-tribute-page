John C. Bogle

Founder and retired CEO of The Vanguard Group

Here's a time line of Jack Bogle's life:

  • 1929 - Born in Verona, New Jersey
  • 1947 - Graduates from Blair Academy cum laude and is accepted to Princeton University where he studies economics and investment
  • 1951 - Graduates from Princeton. Begins working at Wellington Fund
  • 1955 - Is promoted to assistant manager at Wellington Fund
  • 1974 - Founds the Vanguard Company
  • 1975 - Founds the Vanguard 500 Index Fund as the first index mutual fund available to the general public
  • 1996 - Steps down as Vanguard CEO due to heart problems. Returns to Vanguard as Senior Chairman after a successful heart transplant. This leads to conflict between Bogle and his successor, John J. Brennan
  • 1999 - Leaves Vanguard for the Bogle Financial Markets Research Center. Serves on the board to trustees of the National Constitution Center in Philadelphia. Is named one of the investment industry's four "Giants of the 20th Century" by Fortune magazine
  • 2004 - Is named one of the "world's 100 most powerful and influential people" by Time magazine. Receives Institutional Investor's Lifetime Achievement Award.
  • 2005 - Receives an honorary doctorate from Princton

Here are his eight basic rules for simple, common sense investing:

  • Select low-cost index funds
  • Consider carefully the added costs of advice
  • Do not overrate past fund performance
  • Use past performance to determine consistency and risk
  • Beware of stars (as in, star mutual fund managers)
  • Beware of asset size
  • Don't own too many funds
  • Buy your fund portfolio - and hold it

"Don't look for the needle in the haystack. Just buy the haystack!"

Jack Bogle
